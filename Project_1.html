Explore underserved user needs to create digital solutions

To improve ER nurse efficiency, I investigated their journeys to identify areas for enhancement. Through diaries, interviews, and journey mapping, I delved into their needs. Combining qualitative insights with quantitative data from online surveys, I uncovered key worldwide findings. We held ideation workshops with stakeholders to prioritize opportunities and shape an MVP. I then translated these ideas into tangible artifacts for customer evaluation. Additionally, I benchmarked against healthcare innovation trends.

Let me explain a bit my role in the project


I collaborated closely with international project leads, requirements engineers, and software architects. Together, our team aimed to define solutions where nurses can focus more on the patient and less on the technology. We worked at the solution level, meaning that we collaborated with both software and hardware products to define requirements that all products needed to satisfy in order to deliver a unified user experience. Additionally, I was responsible for all user and customer research activities, managing collaboration with external providers, designing and testing the artifacts, supporting in user requirement definition.

Define goals and learning objectives

I organized a stakeholder workshop to define goals, opportunities, hypotheses, learning objectives, and priority markets.

Research plan and recruiting

Planning various research methodologies including internal desk research, online surveys, and interviews, I orchestrated the design of artifacts. Additionally, I coordinated collaborations with partners for recruiting and communication with stakeholders.

Desk Research, Social Listening, and Benchmarking:

I collected and analyzed existing research within the company, conducting interviews with internal subject matter experts. Contextual data was gathered from blogs, social media, and competitor websites. Based on the findings, I refined hypotheses with greater detail and context, began to outline learning objectives, and formulated questions to address with customers.

Online Survey

I formulated and structured survey questions, established participant and market criteria, with the objective of understanding technology usage in hospitals, and the adaptation of POC (Point of Care) devices in the emergency room. Targeting markets in Germany, the UK, the USA, and China, I collaborated with a partner to distribute the online survey and managed the partnership.

Artifacts

Using Figma, I created an animated storyboard to visually present our opportunities. It was used to evaluate the desirability.  Thanks to the collaboration with internal medical professionals, the storyboard closely mirrored reality, as confirmed by interviewees. I outlined macrophases of the user journey to be filled out with customer input and gathered detailed information on their experiences.

Online Interviews

I structured the interviews into three parts: exploring the current user journey, evaluating concepts using the storyboard, and prioritizing concepts based on participant feedback. Utilizing data from online surveys, I tailored questions to specific countries to uncover market differences. The double-blind interviews were conducted by an external partner.I attended them silently, able to message the interviewer for follow-up questions.

Analysis and Data Triangulation

I obtained raw survey data and detailed interview reports, analyzing them alongside quantitative and qualitative data to draft a high-level report. This report was shared with stakeholders to solicit deeper inquiries. Incorporating their input, a final report was created, highlighting key findings, market disparities, and primary insights. Plus, I delivered project personas and ecosystem mapping and service blueprint.

Sharing Reports and Next Steps

I facilitated a workshop with stakeholders to discuss research findings and determine next steps. Presenting results, answering questions, and facilitating discussion on concept prioritization with the input from both customers and stakeholders. Top-rated concepts were translated into four use cases during a co-design session using storyboarding. Ultimately, stakeholders aligned on a unified vision and value proposition for the four use cases, committing to invest further in an MVP and the creation of a promotional video for internal marketing purposes.

Ongoing Collaboration

I collaborated with an external partner to develop the promotional video, and ongoing research on the four use cases is currently underway.
